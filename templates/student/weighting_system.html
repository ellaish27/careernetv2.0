The Weighting System

Admission to public universities in Uganda is determined by a weighted points system. Below is a breakdown of how points are calculated.

1. A'Level Weighting

Your A-Level subjects are categorized into three groups:

Category Weight Factor Description
Essential x3 Core subjects required for the course (usually 2 best subjects). Points: A=6, B=5, C=4, D=3, E=2, O=1, F=0.
Relevant x2 Subjects related to the course but not essential. The best subject is taken.
Desirable x1 Subjects that give a general advantage (e.g., General Paper, Sub-Maths).
Example Calculation (Engineering):
Essential: Math (A=6) + Physics (B=5) = 11 pts.
Weight: 11 x 3 = 33 Weight Points.
2. O'Level Weighting

O-Level results contribute a bonus to your total score.

Grade 1 / A-B 2 / C-D 3-6 / E-F 7-9 / F
Weight 0.3 0.2 0.1 0.0

The aggregate is calculated from the best 10 subjects.
